Even though president elect Donald Trump has vowed to ban Syrian refugees

from entering the country, Massachusetts has quietly been resettling dozens

of refugees within the state. Thanks to data from the Bureau of Population,

Refugees, and Migration Office of Admissions, we can see that Syrian refugees

are mainly being resettled in the poorer cities in Massachusetts, like Lowell

where there are 30 Syrians, or Springfield, where there are 38.
